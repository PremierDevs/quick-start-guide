1. The Big Picture

DealFlow is built around one simple lifecycle:

New Contact → Lead → Qualify → Deal → Close → Stay in Touch → New Deal ↓

The cycle repeats — one Person, many Deals over a lifetime.

Every contact is a Person — and they stay a Person forever. When someone new comes in with buying or selling intent, they enter the Lead phase briefly while you qualify them. Once qualified, you create a Deal and the lead phase ends. But the relationship doesn't.

After the deal closes, that Person stays in your database. You keep in touch — birthdays, anniversaries, quarterly check-ins, holiday cards. When they're ready to buy or sell again (or refer someone), you create a new Deal directly from their Person record. No need to make them a lead again.

Key Concept The lead phase is just the front door — a short qualification step. The Person record is where the lifelong relationship lives. A single Person can have multiple Deals over the years, and every interaction is tracked on that one record.

2. Your Dashboard

The Dashboard (/crm) is your daily starting point. It shows:

KPI Cards

  • Total Leads — your active lead count
  • Active Listings — listing deals in play
  • New Leads (Month) — leads added this month
  • Pending Sales — deals under contract

Pipelines & Tasks

  • Lead Pipeline — leads by status
  • Deal Pipeline — deals by stage with volume & commission
  • My Tasks — overdue (red), today (amber), upcoming
  • Recent Activity — last 5 actions across your contacts

3. Working Your Leads

Lead Statuses

Status What It Means Your Action
Never Contacted Brand new — no outreach yet Call or text within 5 minutes (speed to lead!)
Awaiting Response You've reached out, waiting to hear back Follow up on schedule, try different channels
Ready for Appointment Qualified & ready for a meeting or showing Set the appointment, then convert to a Deal

Daily Lead Workflow

  1. Go to Leads page — click the Never Contacted filter
  2. Click into each lead — review their info, source, and any tracking data
  3. Make contact (call, text, email) and log the activity in the composer
  4. Update their status by working through the qualification checklist
  5. When qualified, click Convert to Client to create a Deal
Tip: Pre-Qualification Checklist Each lead has a checklist on their detail page. Work through it during your conversations — it guides you through the qualifying questions and tracks your progress.

Lead Detail Page

  • Value Strip — source, contact status, lead score, next action
  • Activity Composer — log notes, emails, SMS, and calls
  • Contact Sidebar — phone, email, address, quick-call/email buttons
  • Convert to Client — creates a Deal and links the person
  • Mark as Junk — clears lead status (spam, wrong number, etc.)

4. Managing Deals

Deal Stages

Prospecting → Active → Offer → Under Contract → Closed
Stage What's Happening
Prospecting Client is searching — setting up alerts, showing properties
Active Actively working — listing is live or buyer is seeing homes
Offer Offer submitted, negotiating
Under Contract Binding agreement signed — inspections, appraisal, closing prep
Closed Done! Commission earned.

Deal Detail Page

Main Area

  • Value Strip — price, concessions, commission breakdown, dates
  • Activity Tab — log calls, emails, meetings, showings
  • Tasks Tab — to-dos with due dates and assignments
  • Documents Tab — upload contracts, disclosures, etc.
  • Timeline Tab (under contract+) — closing checklist

Right Sidebar

  • Participants — buyers, sellers, co-buyers linked to deal
  • Property — linked listing with MLS data
  • Vendors — lender, attorney, inspector on the deal
  • Assigned Agent — who owns this deal
Advancing Stages Click the Advance Stage button at the top of a deal. Moving to "Under Contract" opens a special modal for binding date, closing date, and contract details. Moving a listing to "Active" prompts for the MLS number.

5. After the Close — The Real Work

Closing a deal is not the end — it's the start of a lifelong relationship. Your past clients are your best source of future business and referrals.

Stay-in-Touch Milestones

Touchpoint When Why It Matters
Closing Anniversary Annually "Happy home-iversary!" — keeps you top of mind
Birthday Annually Personal touch that builds loyalty
Quarterly Check-In Every 3 months Market updates, neighborhood news, "how's the house?"
Holiday Cards Seasonally Stays visible without being pushy

Be Their Go-To Resource

Your Vendor Directory isn't just for you — it's a service you provide to clients after the sale. Need a plumber? You've got the best one. House cleaner? Here you go. Handyman, electrician, pest control? You have vetted contacts for all of it.

When a past client calls you for a vendor recommendation, that's a touchpoint — a reason to stay connected. Use Refer to Client on any vendor to send their info directly. Every referral you make reinforces that you're not just their agent, you're their real estate resource for life.

The Vendor Advantage Agents who provide ongoing value after the sale get 3–5x more repeat and referral business. Your vendor directory is a competitive edge — use it.

When They're Ready Again

When a past client is ready to buy or sell again — or sends you a referral — you don't need to make them a lead again. Go to their Person record and create a new Deal directly. Their entire history (past deals, notes, activity) is right there on one record.

Tip: Use Smart Lists Create a Smart List like "Past Clients — No Activity 90+ Days" to find people who are due for a check-in. Your database is your business — nurture it.

6. Alerts & Trackers

Found in the Notifications tab on each Deal:

Feature What It Does
Alerts Saved search criteria (price, beds, location, features). The system checks new MLS listings every 15 minutes and emails your client when matches are found.
Trackers Watches a specific MLS listing for price changes, status changes, or new photos. Notifies your client automatically when something changes.

7. People & Smart Lists

The People page shows your full contact database — every lead, past client, vendor contact, and referral.

Smart Lists

Save filtered views for quick access. Examples:

  • "My Active Buyers" — filter: lead status = qualified, source = website
  • "Paris Office Leads" — filter: assigned agent office = Paris
  • "Needs Follow-Up" — filter: last activity > 14 days ago

Smart Lists are available on both the People and Deals pages. You can save them as personal or share with your office/team.

8. Vendors

The Vendor Directory stores service companies you work with — lenders, attorneys, inspectors, title companies, etc.

Finding Vendors

  • Filter by category (Lender, Attorney, etc.)
  • Sort by Most Deals or Most Referrals
  • Search by company or employee name
  • Look for the Preferred badge

Using Vendors on Deals

  • Add vendors to a deal from the deal sidebar
  • Select the specific contact person at that company
  • Use Refer to Client on vendor detail to send a referral
  • Leave Agent Reviews for your team

9. Listings & Properties

Listings (Mini MLS)

Browse all MLS listings synced every 5 minutes from CWTAR and Navica. Use Grid, List, or Map view. Filter by price, beds, baths, acres, county, and 18+ property features.

Toggle My Listings to see only your own.

Properties (Tax Records)

Search 450,000+ county parcel records. Use search flags for precision:

  • owner:Smith — search by owner name
  • address:Maple — search by street
  • city:Paris — search by city
  • apn:001-234 — search by parcel number

10. Tasks

Your task board (/crm/tasks) organizes work into columns:

Column What's In It
Overdue (red) Past-due tasks — handle these first
Today (amber) Due today — your daily priority list
This Week Coming up — plan ahead

Tasks can be created from deal detail pages, or directly from the tasks board. Each task links to a deal and/or person for context.

11. Live Chat

When visitors chat on the website, conversations appear in the Chat Dock at the bottom of your CRM. Chats are routed based on office and floor schedule.

  • Chat Tab — the conversation
  • Activity Tab — the visitor's browsing history, which listings they viewed, UTM source, matched CRM person
  • Click a listing in Activity to see it instantly
Tip: Speed Matters Responding within 60 seconds dramatically increases conversion. Keep the CRM open when you're on floor duty.

13. Help & Navigation Tips

Getting Help

  • Press Esc to close the help drawer

Navigation

  • Sidebar — collapse with the toggle button for more space
  • Dark Mode — toggle in the top nav bar
  • Search — use the global search in the top bar for quick access
  • Customize — click the gear icon at the bottom of the sidebar to show/hide nav items

Quick Reference: Daily Routine

  1. Check Dashboard — review KPIs, overdue tasks, new leads
  2. Work Never Contacted leads — call/text within 5 minutes of assignment
  3. Follow up on Awaiting Response — try different channels (call, text, email)
  4. Convert qualified leads — create Deals and start working the transaction
  5. Advance your deals — log activity, update stages, manage tasks
  6. Set up Alerts & Trackers — keep clients in the loop automatically
  7. Monitor Live Chat — respond fast when you're on floor duty
  8. End of day — check tasks for tomorrow, log any final notes
